For almost 20 years, anti-vaccine advocates have claimed that vaccines cause autism, while medical professionals and an enormous amount of scientific research have shown that they do not. Though there doesn't seem to be any reason to keep debating this issue, some people refuse to let it go.Marco Arturo, a young man at the beginning of his scientific career, decided to tackle the issue head on, claiming that not only do vaccines cause autism, but he had a folder full of rock-solid evidence to prove it.
