The Vaccines for Children program is an entitlement program (a right granted by law) for eligible children, age 18 and below.VFC helps families of children who may not otherwise have access to vaccines by providing free vaccines to doctors who serve them. VFC is administered at the national level by the Centers for Disease Control (CDC) through the National Immunization Program. CDC contracts with vaccine manufacturers to buy vaccines at reduced rates.
